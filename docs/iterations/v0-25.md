# v0.25 — Cashflow Statement per year

**Status:** complete

---

## Goal

Add a **Household Cashflow Statement** section to the Year detail tab — the third and final financial-report-style section. It answers: where did the net surplus go, and how did pot balances change?

The statement reconciles the income statement's net surplus with the change in pot balances from the balance sheet, surfacing:
- contributions to ISA / pension (surplus allocation flows)
- capital movements (pot-to-pot transfers, property sales)
- implied investment return (p50 cross-section residual)

---

## What changes

### UI only — no server changes

All data is already in the response:
- `potPercentiles.byYear[y]` and `[y-1]` — opening and closing real p50 per pot (balance sheet)
- `incomeStatementByYear[y]` — net surplus, capital flows (income statement)
- `incomeStatementByYear[y].capitalIn[]` / `capitalOut[]` — capital movements this year

The cashflow statement is a derived, UI-only computation.

---

## Statement structure

```
Opening net equity  (= prior year balance sheet net equity)
+ Net surplus / (deficit)          [from income statement]
+ Capital in                       [from incomeStatementByYear capitalIn]
− Capital out                      [from incomeStatementByYear capitalOut]
+ Implied investment & growth      [residual = closing equity − opening − above]
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
= Closing net equity               [= this year balance sheet net equity]
```

**Implied investment & growth** is computed as:
```
closing net equity − opening net equity − net surplus − net capital flows
```

It captures:
- investment returns on all pots (stochastic, p50 cross-section)
- property value growth (3% mean real)
- mortgage principal reduction (already reflected in reduced liabilities)
- any residual from the p50 cross-sectional approximation

It is labelled "Implied investment & growth (p50)" in the UI with a tooltip explaining it is the cross-sectional residual.

---

## Year 0 edge case

For the first year in the rail (yearIndex 0), there is no prior year balance sheet. Opening net equity is taken as the sum of initial pot values minus initial mortgage balances — i.e. the scenario's stated starting position. This is deterministic (no simulation involved).

Opening assets at year 0: `sum of pot.initialValue` for all pots.
Opening liabilities at year 0: `sum of pot.mortgage.outstandingBalance` for all mortgaged pots.

These values are available in the `pots` array already passed through route state from `ScenarioScreen`.

## Data mapping

All derived in the UI from existing response fields:

| Line | Source |
|------|--------|
| Opening net equity | `sum(potPercentiles.byYear[y-1].byPot[*].realP50) − sum(liabilities.byYear[y-1].mortgageBalances[*])` — or initial values for year 0 |
| Net surplus | `incomeStatementByYear[y]`: gross income − tax − NI − expenditure |
| Capital in | `sum(incomeStatementByYear[y].capitalIn[*].amount)` |
| Capital out | `sum(incomeStatementByYear[y].capitalOut[*].amount)` |
| Implied investment & growth | `closing − opening − netSurplus − capitalIn + capitalOut` |
| Closing net equity | `sum(potPercentiles.byYear[y].byPot[*].realP50) − sum(liabilities.byYear[y].mortgageBalances[*])` |
